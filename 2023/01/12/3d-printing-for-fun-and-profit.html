3D modelling and printing for fun and profit

I have bought Prusa Mini+ 3D printer a couple of weeks ago. I primarily wanted to use it for printing small things that are hard to produce in other way.

I got rather surprised that my older son Vítek occupied it immediately for printing Lego bricks he needed for his complicated guns. (Do not ask me why he builds guns. I hate it.)

IIRC, Vítek uses Tinkercad’s "Brick mode" for modeling his Lego bricks.

He prints them using PETG material which is perhaps not optimal, but we do not have any other material yet. Larger bricks turned out to be a bit smaller than LEGO originals so there is some tension when they are used in combination. Tikercad brick defaults would perhaps deserve some tuning.

So much about Vítek’s 3D modeling and printing. Now about my own experiments.

My younger son Viliam got a Tamiya CC-01 based RC car as birthday present a couple of weeks ago. It is pretty powerful and fast but its center of gravity is a bit too high so it tends to roll over during sharp turns on rough surfaces.

We realized we needed a solution because we broke one side mirror due to that ☹.

Viliam did not not want to sacrifice maneuverability by limiting the max steering angle. (That would be easy to set up on the transmitter.) Therefore we had to look for another solution.

How about printing offsetted wheel rims thus making the car wider and more stable?

This is how the original ones looked like:

We added ~10 mm offset on each wheel:

I modelled using FreeCAD (dnf install -y freecad on Fedora).

Here are the model files (licensed under Creative Commons with Attribution 4.0):

  • offset-wheel-rim-for-tamiya-cc-01.FCStd - the FreeCAD model file

  • offset-wheel-rim-for-tamiya-cc-01.3mf - a 3mf export with the infill and layer height settings I have used for printing

  • offset-wheel-rim-for-tamiya-cc-01.stl - an STL just in case your tooling likes it better

  • offset-wheel-rim-for-tamiya-cc-01.gcode - for printing on a 3D printer without any tuning

And here is the detailed view of the 3D print. It took roughly 3 hours 50 mins on Prusa Mini+ with 0.15 mm layer height and 30% gyroid infill using PETG filament.

Enjoy!
